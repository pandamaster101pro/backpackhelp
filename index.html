1. Introduction

Backpackhelp is an app that helps students keep track of what's in their backpack. It uses a Bluetooth-connected RFID reader to detect items — like textbooks — and their weight, and it can pull in your class schedule from a calendar feed (such as Canvas) to show what you need to pack for a given day.

Backpackhelp is built and maintained as an independent project, not by a company. This page explains what information the app collects, how it's used, and how you can remove it.

2. Who This App Is For

Backpackhelp is designed for students, and its users may include people under the age of 18. We've written this policy to be as clear and honest as possible for a general audience, including younger users and their parents or guardians. If you're a parent or guardian with questions about your child's use of the app, or you'd like their data removed, contact us at the email address at the bottom of this page.

3. Accounts and Guest Mode

Backpackhelp offers two ways to use the app:

  • With an account — you sign up with an email address and password. Your account saves your profile and scanned items so they're there the next time you open the app.
  • As a guest — you can use the app without creating an account. No profile is created and nothing is saved; any items scanned during a guest session exist only in the app's memory and disappear as soon as the app is closed.

4. Information We Collect

Account information

If you create an account, we collect the email address and password you provide. Your password is handled by Firebase Authentication and is never stored by us in plain text.

Profile information

If you fill in your profile, we store your name, email, year (e.g. grade or academic year), and GPA. These fields are optional and are only visible to you.

Scanned items

When you use the RFID scanner, the names of detected items and your backpack's weight are saved to your account so you can see them again later. This information is only visible to you — Backpackhelp has no feature for sharing it with other users.

Checklists and reminders

Your packing checklists and any reminders you create — including reminder titles, notes, and times — are saved only on your device. They are never uploaded to Firebase or any other server, whether you're signed in or using guest mode.

Calendar and connection settings

If you add a calendar feed URL or connect to a Raspberry Pi RFID reader, those settings — the URL and, if applicable, an API key — are saved only on your device. They are never uploaded to our servers.

5. Device Permissions

  • Bluetooth — used to scan for and connect to your backpack's RFID reader hardware, and to receive the item and weight data it sends.
  • Location (Android only) — Android requires apps to have Location permission in order to perform Bluetooth scans. Backpackhelp does not read, use, or store your GPS location or any other location data — the permission exists solely to satisfy this Android requirement.
  • Camera and microphone — Backpackhelp does not use your camera or microphone, and does not request permission to access either.
  • Local network — used only if you choose to connect to a Raspberry Pi RFID reader over Wi-Fi, as an alternative to a direct Bluetooth connection.
  • Notifications — if you turn on reminders, the app asks for permission to show notifications. These are scheduled entirely on your device (including re-scheduling after a restart); Backpackhelp does not use a push-notification server, and nothing about your reminders leaves your phone.

6. Where Your Information Is Stored

Account and profile data — email, name, year, GPA, and scanned items — is stored using Firebase, a cloud platform operated by Google (Firebase Authentication for login, and Cloud Firestore for your profile and scanned-item data). Firebase is the only third-party service Backpackhelp sends this data to.

Calendar URLs and Raspberry Pi connection settings are stored locally on your device only, and are never sent to Firebase or any other server.

7. How Your Information Is Used

Your information is used only to operate the app for you, specifically to:

  • Sign you in and keep your account secure;
  • Show your saved profile details back to you;
  • Remember what items were scanned so you can view them later;
  • Show your class schedule and packing checklist, based on the calendar feed you provide;
  • Show the reminders you set, at the times you chose — scheduled locally on your device.

We do not sell your information, and we do not share it with advertisers.

8. Deleting Your Account and Data

To permanently delete your account and all associated data:

  1. Open the Profile page in the app.
  2. Tap the menu icon in the top right and choose Delete account.
  3. Confirm your password when prompted.

This permanently deletes your Firebase account and your Firestore profile document, including your saved scanned items. This action cannot be undone.

If you're using guest mode, there's nothing to delete — nothing is stored beyond your current session.

You can also request account deletion by emailing us directly at the address at the bottom of this page.

9. Data Security

We rely on Firebase's built-in security practices — such as encrypted connections and password hashing — to help protect your information. That said, no method of storage or transmission over the internet is 100% secure, and we can't guarantee absolute security.

10. Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, we'll update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to check back occasionally.

Disclaimer

Accuracy

Backpackhelp relies on RFID hardware, Bluetooth connectivity, and third-party calendar feeds (such as Canvas) to detect items, measure weight, and display your schedule. This data can be incomplete, delayed, or inaccurate due to hardware limitations, connectivity issues, or errors in the source calendar feed. We do not guarantee that scan results or schedule information are complete or correct.

Intended Purpose

Backpackhelp is intended as a personal organizational aid to help you keep track of what's in your backpack and what you might need to pack. It is not a substitute for checking your own belongings, and should not be relied on as the sole way of confirming what you have with you.

Responsibility

You are responsible for verifying your own belongings and schedule. Backpackhelp and its developer are not liable for missed items, scheduling errors, or any consequences resulting from reliance on the app's output. The app is provided "as is," without warranties of any kind, express or implied.
